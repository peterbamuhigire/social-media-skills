## 1. The Big Idea

Every presentation must be built around a single Big Idea — a complete sentence with:
- A **point of view** (a stance, not a topic label)
- **What is at stake** for the audience if they do not act

| Weak (label) | Strong (Big Idea with stance) |
|---|---|
| "Campaign proposal" | "This six-week campaign will generate 150 qualified enquiries before the school term begins" |
| "Our credentials" | "In 18 months our clients have generated over UGX 800 million in directly attributed social revenue" |
| "Investment" | "This campaign costs less than acquiring three new customers at your current conversion rate" |

**Slide title discipline:** Every slide title must be a complete assertion, never a label.

## 2. The Sparkline

The structural backbone of the most persuasive presentations:

```
Opening:  WHAT IS (current reality — audience nods)
          ↓ gap created ↓
          WHAT COULD BE (the better future — audience leans forward)

Middle:   Toggle WHAT IS (tension) / WHAT COULD BE (release)
          Each cycle builds the case

Close:    WHAT COULD BE (vivid picture of the new bliss)
          + Call to Action
```

The gap between What Is and What Could Be creates productive tension. Do not resolve it until the call to action.

## 3. The S.T.A.R. Moment

Every great presentation needs one climactic, memorable moment:
- **S — Shocking statistic:** Display the number large. Pause for three seconds.
- **T — Transformative visual:** Emotionally potent imagery that makes the abstract concrete
- **A — Acting it out:** A prop, live demonstration, or physical metaphor
- **R — Relatable anecdote:** A personal or client story with a clear lesson

**One S.T.A.R. moment per deck.** In campaign proposal decks, this belongs on the Opportunity (Slide 2) or Campaign Concept (Slide 5) slide.

---

## 4. Call to Action Taxonomy

Be explicit about which action you need from this specific audience:
- **Doers** — take a physical action (approve, sign, schedule)
- **Suppliers** — provide resources, budget, or access
- **Influencers** — advocate for the recommendation to others
- **Innovators** — generate new ideas in response

---

## 5. The Glance Test

Every slide must be comprehensible to a new viewer in three seconds. If it fails, simplify. The slide anchors the message; the presenter explains it.

---

## 6. Two Strategic Questions (Hatton)

Before preparing any presentation, answer in one sentence each:
1. **Why should they say yes?** — defines content strategy (benefits and differentiation)
2. **Why will they remember this meeting?** — defines delivery strategy (the distinctive moment)
