# Domain Analysis

## Business Development

Strengths:

- Strong coverage of positioning, credentials, proposals, outreach, audit offers, and reactivation.
- Commercially realistic structure for a consultancy trying to win and package work.
- Better than average on offer design and sales-support documentation.

Weaknesses:

- Pricing and offer framing are still heavily EA-oriented.
- Limited built-in guidance for enterprise procurement or multinational stakeholder environments.

Global readiness note:

Usable globally with manual adaptation, but not yet localised enough for confident use across very different buying cultures.

## Onboarding

Strengths:

- Clear intake, audit, persona, and voice-capture logic.
- Good foundation for evidence-led strategy rather than generic recommendations.

Weaknesses:

- Market-specific discovery is not yet mandatory enough.
- Legal, cultural, and internal governance questions should be more explicit for global work.

Global readiness note:

Solid foundation, but needs a stronger market-context intake layer.

## Strategy

Strengths:

- One of the best domains in the repository.
- Strong framework usage and multiple strategy angles beyond generic social planning.
- Good balance between channel, funnel, audience, and content architecture.

Weaknesses:

- Too much dependence on default market assumptions.
- Some strategy recommendations will be locally sensible but globally incomplete without explicit overrides.

Global readiness note:

Potentially premium and globally useful, but only once a context engine is added.

## Planning

Strengths:

- Strong bridge between strategy and execution.
- Content pillars, calendars, campaign briefs, and website content planning are practical and reusable.

Weaknesses:

- Planning outputs may inherit local channel assumptions too easily.
- Localisation of campaign rhythm, seasonality, and buying cycles is underdeveloped.

Global readiness note:

Good structure, moderate global readiness, requires better market inputs.

## Platform Plans

Strengths:

- Broad platform coverage with platform-specific intent.
- Clear evidence that the repository understands that each channel plays a different role.

Weaknesses:

- No formal map of platform relevance by region.
- Some major market-specific platform dynamics are not encoded at system level.

Global readiness note:

Strong channel expertise, moderate global adaptability.

## Execution Playbooks

Strengths:

- Extremely broad and one of the most operationally valuable sections.
- Covers governance, community, SOPs, customer service, crisis response, retention mechanics, and social selling.
- Useful for day-to-day agency discipline.

Weaknesses:

- Many playbooks are operating guides rather than implementation systems.
- Some playbooks reflect EA norms around messaging, service expectations, or conversion flow.

Global readiness note:

Excellent for regional or boutique consultancy operations; not yet universally calibrated.

## Analytics And Reporting

Strengths:

- One of the strongest domains in the repository.
- Includes ROI, revenue planning, testing, metrics frameworks, dashboards, UTM discipline, and sentiment analysis.
- Indicates a business-results mindset rather than vanity reporting.

Weaknesses:

- Cross-market privacy, consent, and attribution variations need more systematic routing.
- Benchmark interpretation by region and sector is not mature enough yet.

Global readiness note:

High potential, especially for strategy-led consulting, but needs stronger international compliance and benchmark calibration.

## AI

Strengths:

- Unusually mature and diversified.
- Covers diagnostics, use cases, workflow design, humanisation, bias, automation, knowledge bases, and policy.
- Strong quality-control orientation.

Weaknesses:

- Global cultural adaptation in AI output is not yet central enough.
- Some AI workflows would benefit from clearer sector and geography-specific safeguards.

Global readiness note:

One of the most globally promising domains, provided cultural and regulatory adaptation gets tighter.

## Policy

Strengths:

- Good awareness of ethics, copyright, provenance, data leakage, and AI risk.
- Stronger than many marketing repositories in legal sensitivity.

Weaknesses:

- Coverage is still selective and not a full regulatory routing system.
- More direct links to sector-specific compliance would improve enterprise readiness.

Global readiness note:

Promising but incomplete for a true global operating system.

## Training

Strengths:

- Supports client enablement and internal capability building.
- Strong practical value for consultancy delivery and handover.

Weaknesses:

- Training material is still regionally framed in parts.
- Less emphasis on multinational team adoption and change management.

Global readiness note:

Useful across markets, but not yet built for multinational transformation work.

## Language And Writing Standards

Strengths:

- Strong consistency in British English and professional register.
- Useful for keeping outputs clean and credible.

Weaknesses:

- British English consistency is good, but global consultancy work needs broader multilingual and cultural flexibility.
- Language quality standards do not yet fully solve localisation or transcreation problems.

Global readiness note:

Good editorial baseline, limited global language system.

## Content Generation

Strengths:

- Helpful downstream execution support for captions, blogs, hashtags, and ideation.
- Benefits from the broader strategic architecture around it.

Weaknesses:

- Should remain downstream of strategy, not become the centre of the system.
- Global adaptation depends on upstream context being stronger than it currently is.

Global readiness note:

Useful supporting layer, but not a differentiator in global consultancy evaluation.

## Overall Domain Pattern

The repository is strongest where strategy, analytics, AI, and operational playbooks intersect. It is weaker where global context must change assumptions automatically. The architecture is already good enough to support world-class work after adaptation upgrades; the missing piece is not more volume, but better market intelligence and routing across the existing domains.
