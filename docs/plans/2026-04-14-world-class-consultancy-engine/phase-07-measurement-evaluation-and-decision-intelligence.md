# Phase 07 - Measurement, Evaluation, And Decision Intelligence

## Objective

Turn analytics, reporting, and evaluation into a world-class decision system rather than a dashboard production function.

## Why This Phase Is Non-Negotiable

The final standard the user asked for includes the ability not only to build plans and strategies but also to evaluate them. That requires stronger benchmark logic, privacy-aware measurement design, commercial interpretation, and clear next-action intelligence.

## Core Outputs

1. A new `global-benchmarking-and-kpi-calibration` skill.
2. Stronger integration across analytics, testing, attribution, ROI, and reporting skills.
3. A standard evaluation framework for strategy reviews, campaign reviews, monthly reports, quarterly reviews, and annual reviews.
4. Stronger business-outcome mapping from social metrics to leads, revenue, trust, retention, and risk.

## Repository Work

### 1. Create benchmark calibration logic

The benchmark skill should help the operator answer:

- what "good" looks like in this market,
- what should be compared against baseline, peer group, or objective,
- how maturity and budget affect interpretation,
- what metrics are misleading in low-data contexts,
- how to distinguish performance noise from strategic signal.

### 2. Retrofit the analytics stack

Priority skills:

- `meta-reporting`
- `meta-roi-framework`
- `meta-testing-framework`
- `meta-utm-tracking`
- `meta-social-metrics-framework`
- `meta-social-marketing-mix-review`
- `meta-analytics-privacy`
- `meta-dashboard-design`
- `meta-revenue-planning`
- `meta-lead-scoring`
- `meta-cohort-analysis`

### 3. Standardise evaluation output patterns

Every evaluation-oriented deliverable should contain:

- objective and context,
- what changed,
- why it changed,
- what the data can and cannot prove,
- commercial interpretation,
- decision recommendation,
- next experiment or action.

### 4. Strengthen executive-facing reporting

Improve deck and report outputs for:

- CEOs,
- CMOs,
- founders,
- boards,
- public-sector leadership,
- procurement-conscious enterprise stakeholders.

## Skills And Files Most Affected

- new: `global-benchmarking-and-kpi-calibration`
- all `meta-*` reporting and measurement skills
- `deck-monthly-report`
- `deck-quarterly-review`
- `deck-annual-review`

## Completion Criteria

- The repository can evaluate strategy and performance with market-aware benchmarks.
- Reports connect activity to business meaning instead of listing metrics.
- Decision quality improves because evaluation outputs recommend what to scale, stop, or redesign.
- Privacy and attribution logic are strong enough for more regulated and international work.

## Risks To Control

- Treating benchmarks as universal when they vary heavily by market and budget.
- Overstating causality from weak attribution evidence.
- Producing reports that are visually polished but strategically empty.

## Reading Material To Buy For This Stage

- *Marketing Metrics* by Paul W. Farris, Neil T. Bendle, Phillip E. Pfeifer, and David J. Reibstein  
  Use it to strengthen metric definitions and commercial interpretation.
- *Web Analytics 2.0* by Avinash Kaushik  
  Use it to improve segmentation, insight extraction, and actionability.
- *Lean Analytics* by Alistair Croll and Benjamin Yoskovitz  
  Use it to sharpen stage-aware KPI selection and decision thresholds.
