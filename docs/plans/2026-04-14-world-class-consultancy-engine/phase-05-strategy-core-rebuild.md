# Phase 05 - Strategy Core Rebuild

## Objective

Refit the repository's core strategy layer so it produces sharper diagnosis, clearer choice, better market adaptation, and more board-level commercial credibility.

## Why This Phase Is The Heart Of The Programme

If the strategy layer remains generic, everything downstream will remain weaker than it should be. This phase is where the repository becomes a real consulting engine rather than an extensive prompt collection.

## Core Outputs

1. Retrofit all flagship strategy skills to consume the context engine and localisation layer.
2. Add stronger "where to play / how to win / what not to do" logic.
3. Add explicit business-case and evaluation design sections to strategy outputs.
4. Create stronger distinction between strategy, tactics, and execution sequence.

## Repository Work

### 1. Retrofit flagship strategy skills

Priority skills:

- `05-social-media-strategy`

### 2. Add strategic diagnosis sections

Every strategy output should answer:

- what the real growth or trust problem is,
- which audiences matter most,
- which channels matter most and why,
- what not to prioritise,
- what trade-offs the client is accepting,
- how business impact will be measured,
- what assumptions could invalidate the plan.

### 3. Add tiered strategy modes

Design variants for:

- SME growth strategy,
- enterprise or institutional strategy,
- market entry strategy,
- turnaround or recovery strategy,
- creator or founder-led strategy,
- public-sector or high-risk communications strategy.

### 4. Add stronger evaluation architecture inside the strategy itself

Each strategy should define:

- north-star outcomes,
- primary and secondary KPIs,
- decision checkpoints,
- test agenda,
- review cadence,
- stop/continue/scale rules.

## Skills And Files Most Affected

- all major strategy skills
- `deck-strategy-presentation`
- `deck-ai-strategy-presentation`
- `deck-initial-pitch`

## Completion Criteria

- Strategy outputs become more selective, differentiated, and commercially defensible.
- Every strategy includes clear choice, sequencing, and evaluation logic.
- Market adaptation is visible in channel role, message framing, budget logic, and risk handling.
- Deck outputs become stronger because the underlying strategy quality improves.

## Risks To Control

- Adding too many frameworks without improving judgement.
- Confusing detail with strategic sharpness.
- Writing strategy documents that cannot be implemented by a real client team.

## Reading Material To Buy For This Stage

- *Marketing Management* by Philip Kotler and Kevin Lane Keller  
  Use it to deepen segmentation, positioning, value, and growth logic.
- *The 1-Page Marketing Plan* by Allan Dib  
  Use it to improve strategic compression and make recommendations easier to operationalise.
- *7 Powers* by Hamilton Helmer  
  Use it to build defensibility and competitive-advantage thinking into higher-level strategy work.
