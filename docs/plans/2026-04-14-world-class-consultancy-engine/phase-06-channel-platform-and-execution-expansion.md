# Phase 06 - Channel, Platform, And Execution Expansion

## Objective

Upgrade platform plans, channel playbooks, and execution systems so they become explicitly market-aware, commercially aligned, and easier to run across client accounts.

## Why This Phase Matters

The repository already has excellent breadth in platform and playbook coverage. The task now is not to multiply channels further. It is to make recommendations more context-sensitive, less assumption-heavy, and more operationally coherent.

## Core Outputs

1. Retrofit platform skills with market-based channel suitability logic.
2. Upgrade playbooks with localisation and governance checkpoints.
3. Add missing orchestration logic between paid, owned, earned, community, and conversion layers.
4. Create clearer execution standards for agencies managing multiple clients.

## Repository Work

### 1. Retrofit platform skills

Priority platform skills:

- `platform-facebook`
- `platform-instagram`
- `platform-linkedin`
- `platform-whatsapp`
- `platform-tiktok`
- `platform-youtube`
- `platform-x-twitter`
- `platform-google-business-profile`
- `platform-podcast-strategy`

Each should state:

- when the platform should lead,
- when it should support,
- when it should be deprioritised,
- what content and conversion role it plays by market,
- what maturity assumptions the recommendation depends on.

### 2. Upgrade execution playbooks

Priority playbooks:

- community management,
- crisis communications,
- paid social,
- customer service,
- social selling,
- WhatsApp business,
- lead magnet system,
- post-click strategy,
- reputation management,
- governance,
- daily operations,
- client retainer management.

Each should include:

- localisation QA,
- escalation triggers,
- cross-functional handoff logic,
- approval path,
- evidence and measurement links,
- resourcing expectations.

### 3. Add integrated execution pathways

Build stronger coordination between:

- content and conversion,
- social and WhatsApp,
- social and email,
- social and PR,
- social and website or landing-page journey,
- social and sales follow-through.

### 4. Improve multi-client operations

Use the repository to define:

- account management rhythm,
- review cadence,
- red-flag escalation,
- approval turnaround rules,
- what gets templated versus customised,
- how quality is maintained under scale.

## Skills And Files Most Affected

- all `platform-*` skills
- all major `playbook-*` skills
- `playbook-agency-operations`
- `playbook-client-retainer-management`
- `playbook-daily-operations-routine`

## Completion Criteria

- Platform recommendations become situational rather than default-driven.
- Playbooks show how work is actually run, not just what "best practice" sounds like.
- The repository can support multi-client delivery with better consistency and lower operational drift.
- Conversion paths are clearer across channels and handoffs.

## Risks To Control

- Allowing channel enthusiasm to outrun business case logic.
- Repeating the same generic advice across all platform skills.
- Under-specifying the handoff from strategy to execution.
