# Phase 08 - AI, Knowledge, And Automation Operating Layer

## Objective

Elevate the repository's already strong AI layer into a more formal operating system for insight generation, quality assurance, reusable knowledge, and carefully governed automation.

## Why This Phase Is Late In The Sequence

The repository already has unusually strong AI coverage. The issue is not "add AI". The issue is to make AI serve the market-adapted consultancy system built in earlier phases, rather than letting it generate fast but context-thin outputs.

## Core Outputs

1. Retrofit AI skills so they consume the new context, localisation, and evaluation layers.
2. Strengthen the relationship between AI tools and the consultancy knowledge base.
3. Build clearer human-in-the-loop quality patterns.
4. Formalise where automation is safe, where review is mandatory, and where AI should not be used.

## Repository Work

### 1. Retrofit key AI skills

Priority skills:

- `ai-readiness-diagnostic`
- `ai-use-case-mapping`
- `ai-strategy-co-thinker`
- `ai-rag-brand-knowledge-base`
- `ai-content-humaniser`
- `brand-voice-ai-training`
- `playbook-ai-content-workflow`
- `playbook-ai-automation-workflow`
- `ai-cultural-bias-audit`
- `ai-agentic-marketing-workflows`
- `ai-generative-search-optimisation`

### 2. Create a consultancy knowledge system

The knowledge system should organise:

- market assumptions,
- sector notes,
- approved frameworks,
- brand voice rules,
- case studies,
- benchmark references,
- prompt patterns,
- evaluation lessons,
- legal and governance notes.

### 3. Strengthen HITL controls

Define mandatory review points for:

- culturally sensitive messaging,
- regulated sectors,
- public claims,
- pricing proposals,
- crisis communications,
- AI-generated insights presented as fact,
- automated responses to prospects or customers.

### 4. Upgrade AI from generation to augmentation

Ensure the repo uses AI to:

- accelerate research and scenario design,
- stress-test strategies,
- support content transformation,
- improve knowledge retrieval,
- surface risks and alternatives,
- support evaluation and iteration.

Avoid treating AI as a replacement for strategic responsibility.

## Skills And Files Most Affected

- all flagship AI skills
- AI policy skills
- strategy and content skills that integrate AI guidance

## Completion Criteria

- AI outputs become more context-aware and less generic.
- The repository has a clearer knowledge architecture for repeated consulting use.
- Human review is enforced where trust, legality, or brand risk matters most.
- AI becomes a systematic differentiator for the consultancy rather than a loose collection of AI prompts.

## Risks To Control

- Expanding automation into trust-sensitive work without adequate safeguards.
- Allowing AI convenience to dilute strategic and cultural precision.
- Building a knowledge base that becomes a dumping ground instead of a curated asset.

## Reading Material To Buy For This Stage

- *AI Engineering* by Chip Huyen  
  Use it to strengthen system design, evaluation, and operational thinking around AI-enabled workflows.
- *The AI Marketing Canvas* by Raj Venkatesan and Jim Lecinski  
  Use it to align AI opportunities to real customer moments and business outcomes.
- *Co-Intelligence* by Ethan Mollick  
  Use it to sharpen practical human-plus-AI operating discipline inside the consultancy.
