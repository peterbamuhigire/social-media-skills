# Design: AI Skills Enhancement — 12-Book Synthesis
**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Author:** Peter (via Claude Code)
**Status:** Approved

## Overview

Enhance the social-media-skills AI suite using insights extracted from 12 books on AI in marketing and business management. The work covers 16 skill files: 7 existing skills strengthened and 9 new skills created, organised into 3 thematic batches.

**Source books:**
- Ltifi, M. (ed.) (2025) *Advances in Digital Marketing in the Era of AI* (×2 volumes). CRC Press.
- Boustany, S. (2024) *Generative AI for Social Media Marketing*.
- Nayebi, F. (2025) *Foundations of Agentic AI for Retail*. Gradient Divergence.
- Sweenor, D.E. and Mulkers, Y. (2024) *Generative AI Business Applications*. TinyTechMedia.
- Erné, J. (2024) *The AI Handbook for Management Consultants*.
- Roth, H. and neuroflash Team (2024) *AI Strategy 2025 for Marketing Teams*.
- Farri, E. and Rosani, G. (2025) *HBR Guide to Generative AI for Managers*. HBR Press.
- Johnsen, M. (2024) *AI in Digital Marketing*. Mercury Learning.
- Venkatesan, R. and Lecinski, J. (2026) *The AI Marketing Canvas*, 2nd edn. Stanford University Press.
- Randazzo, G.W. (2024) *Winning Marketing Strategies Using Generative AI*. Business Expert Press.
- Lamplugh, M. (2024) *The AI Marketing Playbook*, 2nd edn. Mercury Learning.

## Approach: Thematic Batches (Option C)

Three sequential batches, each committable as a unit:

1. **Batch 1** — Strengthen 7 existing AI skills with new frameworks
2. **Batch 2** — Create 5 new operational AI skills
3. **Batch 3** — Create 4 new frontier AI skills

## Batch 1: Foundations Upgrade (Strengthen Existing Skills)

### 1. `ai-readiness-diagnostic`
**Add:**
- Venkatesan's 2×2 Use Case Matrix: Productivity vs Growth × Internal vs External
- Nayebi's three-wave maturity labels: Wave 1 (Automation), Wave 2 (Predictive ML), Wave 3 (Agentic AI)
- Map each diagnostic item to a wave level

### 2. `ai-marketing-canvas-assessment`
**Add:**
- Canvas Step 5 (Monetisation): AI as competitive advantage and new revenue source
- Agile sprint methodology for AI marketing iteration (monthly sprints, not quarterly reviews)
- AI-to-AI marketing section: machine-readable brand content for AI agent decision-making
- Four Customer Relationship Moments: Acquisition, Retention, Growth, Advocacy

### 3. `prompt-engineering-library`
**Add:**
- Erné's 5-Step Perfect Prompt: Role → Context → Task → Format → Constraints
- Roth's 8 Golden Rules of prompting
- Role-prompting as the single highest-impact technique (with EA market examples)
- MVOSSTE prompting workflow (Randazzo): Mission → Vision → Objective → Situation → Strategy → Tactics → Execution
- Prompt footnoting as a professional practice

### 4. `ai-content-humaniser`
**Add:**
- Content recycling pipeline: 1 asset → 10 platform-ready variants
- Platform adaptation checklist (tone, format, length per platform)
- AI spam detection awareness: quality bar is rising, human layer becomes more valuable

### 5. `playbook-ai-automation-workflow`
**Add:**
- Zapier/Make.com no-code automation stack: connect AI tools to existing workflows
- Multi-agent architecture primer: specialised agents in concert (research + copy + analytics + scheduling)
- HITL (Human-in-the-Loop) escalation protocol: routine decisions automated, high-risk decisions escalated
- AutoGen/multi-agent orchestration introduction

### 6. `ai-vendor-evaluation`
**Add:**
- RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) tools category
- Synthetic research tools: Supernatural AI, Glimpse, CustomGPT
- Agentic AI tools: OfferFit, Braze, Persado
- EA accessibility and cost filters for each new category

### 7. `training-ai-foundations`
**Add:**
- Co-Pilot vs Co-Thinker distinction (HBR): speed tasks vs reflection-heavy strategy tasks
- Three-wave AI evolution framework: Automation → Predictive ML → Agentic
- Structured dialogue outlines vs single prompts: when to use each

## Batch 2: New Operational Skills

### 8. `ai-whatsapp-chatbot-design` *(new)*
Design WhatsApp LLM chatbots for EA markets.

**Covers:**
- Social presence principles: warmth, local greetings, empathy markers
- Trust-building in conversational AI (Ltifi Chapter 15 findings)
- FAQ mapping → decision tree → LLM layer architecture
- HITL escalation: define containment rate threshold, escalation triggers
- EA-first language: colloquial registers, Luganda/Swahili greeting options
- Transparency: disclose AI nature per Uganda Data Protection and Privacy Act (2019)
- Measurement: containment rate, CSAT, resolution time, escalation rate
- Tool options: Twilio, WATI, Respond.io, Meta Cloud API

### 9. `ai-content-recycling-pipeline` *(new)*
Turn one content asset into 10 platform-ready variants.

**Covers:**
- Master content brief → blog/long-form → AI extraction of 5 key points
- Output map: 5 social captions (FB/IG/LI/TikTok/X) + carousel outline + Reel/Short script + email snippet + quote card + audio/podcast outline
- Platform formatting guide: character limits, aspect ratios, tone adjustments per platform
- AI tool at each step: Claude/ChatGPT (extraction), Canva Magic (visuals), Lumen5/Descript (video/audio)
- Quality gate: human review checklist before any variant is published
- Time benchmark: one 1,000-word blog → 10 variants in under 60 minutes

### 10. `ai-rag-brand-knowledge-base` *(new)*
Build a client-specific RAG knowledge base for accurate, on-brand AI output.

**Covers:**
- What to include: brand guide, audience personas, past campaigns, product/service info, tone-of-voice examples, competitor landscape
- How to structure documents for LLM retrieval (chunking, metadata tagging)
- Tool options: Notion AI, CustomGPT, ChatGPT Projects, Claude Projects, Mem.ai
- Query workflow: how content creators should interact with the knowledge base
- Maintenance protocol: quarterly review and update cycle
- EA relevance: building local market context into the knowledge base

### 11. `ai-synthetic-personas` *(new)*
Generate research-grade audience personas without primary fieldwork.

**Covers:**
- When to use synthetic vs primary research
- Structured prompt template: demographics + income + platform behaviour + pain points + goals + media habits
- Uganda/EA calibration: default demographics, income bands (UGX), platform usage norms
- Validation checklist: cross-reference with available secondary data before using in strategy
- 3-persona output format: Persona Name, Archetype, Day in the Life, Content preferences, Messaging triggers, Platforms, Barriers
- Synthetic focus group technique: prompt AI to simulate audience reactions to campaign concepts
- Citation standard: always disclose synthetic origin in deliverables

### 12. `ai-strategy-co-thinker` *(new)*
Use AI as a thought partner throughout strategy development.

**Covers:**
- Co-Pilot vs Co-Thinker distinction: when to use each mode
- Multi-step dialogue sequence for brand strategy: context brief → stakeholder mapping → pain point table → red flags → strategic options → recommendation
- MVOSSTE workflow with AI at each stage (Randazzo): prompts for Mission, Vision, Objective, Situation Analysis, Strategy, Tactics, Execution
- Job-to-be-Done framing for social media strategy: "what job is our audience hiring this content to do?"
- Risk mapping workflow: list campaign assumptions → prioritise critical ones → design rapid tests
- Prompt footnoting: document AI-generated outputs with the prompts used
- Quality gate: AI generates options, human consultant selects and refines

## Batch 3: New Frontier Skills

### 13. `ai-agentic-marketing-workflows` *(new)*
Design and implement autonomous marketing agent systems.

**Covers:**
- PRAL loop: Perceive–Reason–Act–Learn (foundational architecture)
- BDI model for marketing: Beliefs (audience data) → Desires (business goals) → Intentions (campaign tactics)
- OODA cycle: Observe–Orient–Decide–Act for real-time marketing decisions
- Three-wave maturity roadmap: where clients sit and how to progress
- Five agentic workflow templates: content pipeline agent, sentiment monitoring agent, proactive campaign agent, multi-agent reporting system, dynamic response agent
- HITL safeguards: define decision thresholds, escalation protocols, human override mechanisms
- Multi-agent orchestration: research agent + copywriting agent + analytics agent + scheduling agent
- EA implementation realism: Wave 1–2 is achievable now; Wave 3 requires investment
- Tool options: Claude API, ChatGPT Assistants API, AutoGen, n8n, Zapier AI

### 14. `ai-generative-search-optimisation` *(new)*
Optimise content for AI-native search engines and LLM citation.

**Covers:**
- GEO vs SEO: what changes when ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini become discovery tools
- LLM citation principles: factual accuracy, clear attribution, well-structured prose, concise summaries
- Content structure for AI-native search: headers, bullet points, definitions, FAQ sections
- Machine-readable brand content: structured data, explicit brand values, clear pricing, parseable product information
- AI-to-AI marketing: when consumers' AI agents filter content before humans see it
- GEO content audit framework: assess existing content for LLM-readability
- EA relevance: forward-looking but directionally important as AI tool adoption grows
- Measurement: track brand mentions in AI-generated search results (manual monitoring protocol)

### 15. `ai-predictive-analytics-social` *(new)*
Apply predictive analytics to social media strategy and content decisions.

**Covers:**
- Five predictive use cases: follower churn, content performance, audience personalisation, social commerce forecasting, cross-sell identification
- RFM analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) applied to social media audiences
- CLV (Customer Lifetime Value) modelling for social media communities
- AutoML for segmentation: tools for non-data-science teams (Obviously AI, Akkio, Pecan AI)
- Predictive content calendar: use historical engagement data to predict optimal content mix
- Five-step data science workflow: Collect → Clean → Analyse → Implement → Monitor
- Personalisation ROI measurement: 7-step framework (KPIs → A/B test → CLV impact → CSAT → cost-benefit → review)
- EA calibration: data sources available in Uganda (Meta Business Suite, Google Analytics, WhatsApp Business stats)

### 16. `ai-data-foundation-audit` *(new)*
Audit and prepare client data for AI marketing readiness.

**Covers:**
- Why data quality is always Step 1 (Venkatesan's Canvas, Lamplugh, Ltifi — all three converge)
- Data hygiene checklist: duplicates, missing fields, inconsistencies, format standardisation
- EA client reality: data typically fragmented across WhatsApp, Facebook, email, spreadsheets
- Data mapping framework: identify all customer data sources → consolidate into unified customer profile
- Governance framework: who owns data, access controls, retention policy, Uganda DPA 2019 compliance
- 30-day remediation plan: prioritised action list from audit findings
- Tool options: Google Sheets (starter), Airtable, HubSpot Free, Notion (accessible for EA clients)
- Handoff: how to connect cleaned data to AI tools (RAG knowledge base, segmentation tools, chatbots)

## Constraints and Standards

All 16 skill files must comply with CLAUDE.md:
- SKILL.md only — no README, CHANGELOG, or auxiliary docs
- 500-line hard limit — detailed references go in `references/` subfolder
- British English throughout
- Required Input section — client name, industry, country/city, primary goal
- Quality Criteria section — 5–8 bullets per skill
- Uganda/East Africa default market
- Frontmatter: `name` and `description` only

---

## Success Criteria

- All 7 existing skills are noticeably richer and cite new book sources
- All 9 new skills are original (not duplicates of existing skills)
- Each new skill has a clear, non-overlapping scope
- Every skill references at least 2 of the 12 source books
- No skill exceeds 500 lines
- All skills pass a quality read: would a Ugandan marketing consultant find this immediately actionable?
