# Gap Analysis — Social Media Skills Suite
**Date:** March 2026
**Scope:** 46 skills across business development, strategy, platform, playbook, analytics, training, and utility categories
**Context:** Uganda/East Africa primary market
**Sources:** 8 books across two research batches (see bibliography)

---

## Executive Summary

**Finding 1 — AI is not yet a first-class workflow.**
The suite has no dedicated AI workflow skill. Both batches independently flagged this as the single highest-priority gap. Clients and practitioners need step-by-step guidance on prompt engineering, quality control, GenAI tool selection, and AI content ethics — none of which exist as standalone or embedded content anywhere in the suite.

**Finding 2 — Attribution and measurement are fragmented.**
UTM tracking, A/B testing, and platform-specific analytics interpretation are absent as structured workflows. The existing `meta-roi-framework` and `meta-reporting` skills cover principles but lack the operational mechanics (UTM naming conventions, GA4 integration, test hypothesis design) that turn data into decisions.

**Finding 3 — The suite is platform-heavy but channel-thin.**
The suite has seven platform skills yet no coverage of SMS/WhatsApp broadcast marketing, chatbots, or Google Business Profile — three channels with outsized relevance for East African SMEs who rely on mobile-first, proximity-based, and review-driven discovery. Social listening also has no home despite being referenced across multiple existing skills.

**Finding 4 — Owned media and resilience are underserved.**
No skill addresses platform-agnostic resilience (owned vs. rented audiences), email list-building from social, or online reputation management. For Ugandan clients operating in low-trust digital environments, the Google Business Profile and review generation gap is particularly acute.

**Finding 5 — Community and advocacy are missing operational layers.**
Employee advocacy, UGC strategy, and chatbot/conversational marketing represent high-ROI tactics for SME clients but have no playbook. The PESO model — a unifying framework that ties paid, earned, shared, and owned channels together — is also absent, leaving strategy skills disconnected from each other.
