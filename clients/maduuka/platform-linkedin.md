## 1. Company Page vs Personal Profile Strategy

**Run both in parallel.** Maduuka is a B2B SaaS product in an early growth stage. The founder's personal network is the primary lead source in Phase 1. At the same time, business owners researching a software purchase will look for a Company Page — its absence signals immaturity or risk.

**Maduuka Company Page role:**
- Institutional credibility — it confirms Maduuka is a real company, not a WhatsApp-based service
- EFRIS and product-specific content that would be too promotional on Peter's personal profile
- Document carousels and sector-specific case studies
- LinkedIn Events (when follower count reaches 150+ for LinkedIn Live)

**Peter Bamuhigire personal profile role:**
- Primary lead generation engine in Phase 1 — his existing connections include the exact business owner and professional audience Maduuka needs
- Thought leadership: EFRIS expertise, SME business management, founder journey
- First-person storytelling that builds the know-like-trust sequence
- Amplification of Maduuka Company Page posts through sharing

**Cross-amplification rule:** When Maduuka Company Page publishes a strong post (EFRIS checklist carousel, sector-specific case study), Peter shares it to his personal network with a 1–2 line personal comment adding context. This extends Company Page reach to his network before the Page has built its own follower base.

## 4. Content Strategy for LinkedIn

Apply the Know, Like and Trust framework alongside the content pillar structure:

**On Peter's personal profile:**
- Monday: Thought leadership — EFRIS insight, stock management lesson, SME business observation (The Business Owner's Edge)
- Tuesday: Client story or founder behind-the-scenes (Real Business Stories or Inside Maduuka)
- Wednesday: Question or poll — invite discussion, respond to every comment
- Thursday: Practical how-to — a specific actionable framework or checklist (The Business Owner's Edge)

**On Maduuka Company Page:**
- Tuesday: EFRIS or compliance carousel (The Business Owner's Edge)
- Thursday: Product feature or sector-specific walkthrough (Inside Maduuka)
- Friday: Case study or client spotlight when available (Real Business Stories)

**Algorithm fundamentals for LinkedIn:**
- External links go in the first comment, not the post body — LinkedIn penalises off-platform links with reduced distribution. Add "(link in first comment)" at the end of every post with a URL.
- Comments are worth 2× likes to the algorithm; shares are worth 3× likes. Prioritise getting comments over likes — end posts with a genuine question.
- The first 60 minutes after publishing are critical. Alert one or two trusted contacts to engage immediately after publishing to trigger broader distribution.
- Accept pending connection requests immediately after publishing — new connections see the most recent post on their feed, extending reach to fresh eyes.

**Post structure (AIDA — Pattar, 2022):**
```
[ATTENTION — Hook line. One sentence. Must earn the "see more" tap.]

[INTEREST — Lines 2–3: add context without resolving the hook yet.]

[DESIRE — Body: develop the idea toward the payoff. One continuous flow.]

[ACTION — Payoff + CTA: the specific outcome or insight, followed by one question to invite comments.]

[#Maduuka — signature hashtag on every post from Peter's account]
```

Use white space between every 1–3 lines. Walls of text are scrolled past.

## 5. B2B Lead Generation Approach

Maduuka's LinkedIn lead generation targets three buyer profiles:

**Patrick (retail owner):** Less likely to be on LinkedIn; if reached here, will be in SME owner or entrepreneur groups. Content angle: stock management and EFRIS compliance.

**Dr. Joseph (pharmacist):** LinkedIn-active. Follows pharmaceutical industry and business management content. Content angle: NDA and EFRIS compliance, professional-grade systems, pharmacy-specific case studies. Reach via: Pharmacy Board of Uganda community, healthcare professional groups, Uganda Pharmaceutical Association contacts.

**Business managers and procurement officers at larger SMEs:** These are not the primary 90-day target but represent Phase 2 opportunities. Reach via: company targeting (ABM strategy), sponsored content targeting by job title (Operations Manager, General Manager, Finance Manager) in Uganda.

**Five-step organic lead generation system:**

1. Peter publishes EFRIS/stock management insight post targeting the buyer's specific pain point
2. At the end of value-led posts: "Send me a message and I'll share the full checklist / framework" — creates a reason to DM without feeling like a sales pitch
3. When a decision-maker engages with a post, send a connection request within 48 hours with a personalised note referencing their engagement
4. Once connected, if engagement history is strong, send a warm DM: "I noticed you've engaged with our EFRIS content a few times. Are you currently dealing with this for your business? Happy to share what we've built."
5. Hard CTA maximum once per week: "If you're a [target client] dealing with [problem], here's the free trial link."

**Content ACT Framework (Dodaro, 2019):**
- **Authority** — EFRIS guides, stock management frameworks, Peter's first-person market observations
- **Credibility** — client case studies with specific numbers, demo results, product demonstrations
- **Trust** — founder behind-the-scenes, the build story, honest product limitations acknowledged alongside strengths

Target: Peter's first-degree connections who are SME owners, business managers, or professionals in retail, hospitality, healthcare, or general business in Uganda. **Maduuka Company Page:** 3 posts per week (Tuesday, Thursday, Friday). Never post more than once per day on the Company Page — it suppresses each post's reach.

**Peter's personal profile:** 4 posts per week (Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday). Friday is reserved for a rest day or an opportunistic post if something newsworthy occurs.

**Best posting times (EAT, UTC+3):**
- Monday–Thursday, 7–9am: Pre-work professional audience active
- Tuesday–Thursday, 12–1pm: Lunch break engagement window
- Wednesday, 5–6pm: Post-work professional catchup
