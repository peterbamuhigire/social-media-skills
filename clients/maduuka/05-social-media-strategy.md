## 1. Situation Analysis

### Market Context

Uganda's SME sector is digitising unevenly. Mobile money penetration is near-universal; WhatsApp is the primary business communication channel across all income levels; Android smartphones are the dominant device. Yet business management software adoption among small businesses remains low — not because owners do not want it, but because the available options are either built for Western markets (Sage, QuickBooks, Odoo) or are single-developer custom systems that create dependency rather than solving it. Uganda Revenue Authority's EFRIS rollout is accelerating, creating a compliance urgency that no available product addresses with clarity and simplicity for the SME market. The gap between the compliance requirement and the available tools is Maduuka's primary entry point.

### Key Competitors

The real competitive set is not the global SaaS brands. It is the informal alternatives that currently hold the market:

**Local developer / IT consultant custom systems**
Active on: Personal LinkedIn profiles, occasional Facebook business pages. Posting frequency: irregular, personal. Strength: deep client relationship, WhatsApp support. Weakness: single-person dependency; no roadmap; client is stranded when the developer moves on.

**Pirated and unlicensed desktop POS software**
Not a brand competitor in the social media sense — these are not running content strategies. They hold market share through price (zero) and familiarity. Their weakness is structural: no updates, no backup, no compliance, no mobile.

**EFRIS configuration consultants**
Active on LinkedIn (individual profiles) and Facebook. Posting frequency: low. Strength: solve a live compliance pain point. Weakness: solve only that one problem and leave the rest of the business running manually.

*Global brand comparators (ERPNext, Sage Africa, Loyverse, Odoo) are findable by name on LinkedIn and Facebook with active company pages. They are not the daily decision these SME owners are making, but they establish a category expectation.*

### Current Digital Presence Summary

**Status: Zero.** Maduuka has no active social media accounts on any platform. This is a clean-slate launch.

**Adjacent brand assets:**
- Peter Bamuhigire: existing personal accounts with established credibility
- Chwezi Core Systems: existing company accounts

**Gap:** The product brand (Maduuka) has no digital footprint independent of the founder and parent company. All audience-building must begin from zero.

**Opportunity:** Starting from zero with a clear strategy is better than inheriting a poorly-built presence. No legacy content to clean up, no wrong expectations to correct, no audience that was acquired under a different value proposition.

**Waitlist asset:** Two warm prospects (a bar and a pharmacy). These are the first two conversions before any social content goes live.

### Audience Overview

*See 03-audience-personas for full persona cards.*

**Patrick** (The Stretched Retailer) — Most active on Facebook and WhatsApp. Motivated by stock control certainty and EFRIS compliance relief. Wants to be spoken to as a capable business owner, not a technology laggard.

**Amina** (The Restaurant Owner Under Pressure) — Most active on Instagram and Facebook. Motivated by operational clarity at closing time and profit visibility. Already on the waitlist — highest-priority conversion in Phase 1.

**Dr. Joseph** (The Professional Pharmacist) — Most active on LinkedIn and WhatsApp. Motivated by compliance defensibility and the systems foundation for expansion. Willing to pay for quality; expects professional-grade content in return.

**Platform overlap:** Patrick and Amina both use Facebook. Facebook is the one platform where targeted reach across both personas is achievable from a single boosted post.

## 2. Strategy Statement

Maduuka's social media strategy for the first 90 days is a client acquisition programme, not a brand awareness exercise. The platform is live on web and Android. The goal is 20 paying clients before the iOS launch. Every piece of content must have a visible line of sight to a business owner taking action — booking a demo, starting a trial, or sending a message. We are not building an audience and hoping sales follow. We are using content to identify and qualify business owners who are experiencing the specific problems Maduuka solves — stock leakage, EFRIS anxiety, manual chaos at closing time — and giving them a credible, specific, low-friction reason to try the product.

The strategy runs across three owned accounts: Peter Bamuhigire's personal LinkedIn and Instagram (thought leadership and human face), and the Maduuka product accounts (direct lead generation). The founder's personal accounts have a reach advantage early; the Maduuka product accounts build independent audience over time. Both are required. Maduuka's positioning in the social space is the knowledgeable, honest, built-for-here alternative to the custom developer — not the affordable version of Sage.

## 3. Platform Selection Rationale

### LinkedIn — Primary

**Priority:** Primary
**POEM classification:** Owned (organic Company Page and Peter's personal profile) + Earned (shares, comments, recommendations) + Paid (selective sponsored posts)
**Platform role:** B2B lead generation, founder thought leadership, professional credibility with Dr. Joseph (pharmacist) persona and business-owner decision-makers. The only platform where Maduuka can reach a professional who will actively research a software solution before buying.

**Rationale:** Dr. Joseph is LinkedIn-active. Business owners making purchase decisions for professional services increasingly check LinkedIn before deciding. Peter's existing professional presence gives Maduuka an amplification advantage from day one. LinkedIn's professional context also matches the brand's sharp, grounded voice better than entertainment-first platforms.

**EA behaviour note:** LinkedIn usage in Uganda and East Africa is growing among formal sector professionals, NGO workers, and educated business owners. It is not the mass-market platform (that is Facebook), but it reaches decision-makers who are not reachable elsewhere.

---

### Instagram — Primary

**Priority:** Primary
**POEM classification:** Owned (organic product account) + Earned (shares, tags)
**Platform role:** Mid-funnel visual brand building; reaching Amina (hospitality) and younger business owners (28–38 age band); product demonstration through short Reels; building a consistent, credible visual presence for the product brand.

**Rationale:** Amina is already on the waitlist and is Instagram-active in the hospitality context. Instagram Reels are currently one of the highest-reach organic formats available. Visual content showing a Kampala shop dashboard or a restaurant closing-time report will perform better here than on any other platform. The demographic overlap with Maduuka's primary buyer persona is strong.

---

### Facebook — Secondary

**Priority:** Secondary (paid-boosted primary; organic secondary)
**POEM classification:** Owned (organic Page) + Earned (shares, group mentions) + Paid (lead generation ads)
**Platform role:** Broadest reach to Patrick (retailer) persona; SME owner Facebook groups in Uganda; paid lead generation targeting by business type and geography.

**Rationale:** Facebook reaches the widest cross-section of Ugandan business owners. It is where Patrick spends his evenings. It is where SME business groups operate. Organic Facebook Page reach is limited without paid support (typically 2–5% of followers for a new page), so Facebook strategy in Phase 1 is primarily about paid lead generation ads, not organic brand building.

---

### X / Twitter — Secondary

**Priority:** Secondary
**POEM classification:** Owned (organic account) + Earned (retweets, mentions)
**Platform role:** EFRIS and regulatory commentary; business tech discourse; reaching journalists and sector commentators; building brand voice authority in short-form professional discourse.

**Rationale:** X in Uganda is used by journalists, business commentators, and opinion leaders — not mass-market SME owners. Maduuka's presence here builds category credibility and may generate press mentions. Low time investment required; 2–3 posts per week from the Maduuka account or Peter's personal account are sufficient.

---

### WhatsApp — Always-on

**Priority:** Always-on (not a content platform; a conversion and retention channel)
**POEM classification:** Owned
**Platform role:** Demo follow-up, trial onboarding, existing client support, warm-prospect nurturing.

**Note:** WhatsApp Business should be set up before the first social post goes live. Every conversion from social media will route through WhatsApp — it is the closing channel, not the acquisition channel.

---

### YouTube — Deferred

**Priority:** Out of scope for Phase 1
**Note:** Long-form tutorial content and product walkthroughs are a strong Phase 2 priority, particularly for Dr. Joseph (who searches YouTube for compliance guidance). Defer until the product and team have the capacity to produce consistent video content.

### ARM Content Testing Lens (Hahn, 2003)

Before approving any content plan, apply the ARM test:

| Letter | Objective | Maduuka Phase 1 check |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Attract** | Bring in new audiences | EFRIS education content, problem-naming posts, boosted Facebook ads — all attract people who do not yet know Maduuka |
| **R — Retain** | Keep existing followers engaged | Weekly industry insight, behind-the-scenes content, product updates — content that rewards following |
| **M — Motivate** | Drive action | Demo CTAs, trial offers, specific "book a demo" posts — once per week maximum to avoid promotional overload |

A Phase 1 plan heavy on M (conversion content) without A or R will produce short-term noise and long-term audience shrinkage. The mix must be balanced: primarily A and R content with one M moment per week.

## 4. Brand Voice Summary

Maduuka sounds like the most useful person in the room — direct, grounded in Ugandan business reality, and never wasting a word. The brand's three tone words are Sharp, Grounded, and Empowering. Sharp means one idea per post and no corporate filler. Grounded means named Ugandan problems, real EA context, and no aspirational distance from the audience's daily reality. Empowering means the business owner is always the protagonist — Maduuka is the tool; the client is the hero. On LinkedIn, the tone is professional and peer-level. On Instagram, it is visual and immediate. On Facebook, it is warm and community-facing. On WhatsApp, it is personal and direct. The brand would never say "world-class," "revolutionary," or "cutting-edge." It would say "your closing time report in three minutes." See 04-brand-voice-intake for the complete guide.

## 5. Content Pillars

*See 10-content-pillars for full pillar cards. Summary below.*

Apply the 10-4-1 rule (Bodnar and Cohen, 2012): for every 15 posts, 10 share general value (EFRIS guides, business tips, industry observation), 4 are original Maduuka brand content (product posts, case studies, team content), and 1 is directly promotional (demo CTA, trial offer, pricing post).

**Recommended three pillars for Phase 1:**

| Pillar | Purpose | % of mix | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Business Owner's Edge | Educational content that makes the reader a more informed business owner — EFRIS guides, stock management tips, business finance basics | 40% | Hub |
| Real Business Stories | Social proof and case studies featuring Ugandan business owners; client wins; before/after narratives | 30% | Hub/Hero |
| Inside Maduuka | Product demonstrations, feature explainers, team content, company news | 30% | Hygiene/Hero |

Promotional content (CTAs for demos and trials) is distributed across all three pillars within the 10-4-1 ratio — not a separate pillar.

## 7. Community Management Principles

**Principle 1 — Response time SLAs**
Comments on all platforms: respond within 4 business hours. Direct messages and WhatsApp enquiries: respond within 2 hours during business hours (8am–7pm EAT). Complaints or unhappy feedback: respond within 1 hour. A new business with zero reviews cannot afford a single unanswered comment — the comment itself is the trust signal.

**Principle 2 — Tone in responses**
All responses use the brand voice: Sharp, Grounded, Empowering. Do not become more formal when responding to challenges — that signals defensiveness. Do not become more casual when responding to compliments — that undermines credibility. Maintain the same register across all reactive communication.

**Principle 3 — Escalation protocol**
Any enquiry involving billing, data security, a product bug, or a client complaint about the product is escalated immediately to the founder (Peter) via WhatsApp within 30 minutes of receipt. Do not attempt to resolve technical or billing issues in public comment threads — acknowledge, thank, and move to DM within the first reply.

**Principle 4 — Negative comment policy**
Respond publicly to negative comments with one short, professional acknowledgement. Invite the person to move the conversation to DM or WhatsApp for resolution. Never delete a negative comment unless it violates community standards (abusive language, spam, off-topic promotion). A visible public resolution to a complaint is more valuable to the brand than the absence of the complaint.

**Principle 5 — UGC and mentions**
Any client who posts about Maduuka or tags the brand should be acknowledged publicly within the same day. Client posts with specific results (dashboard screenshots, business wins) are the highest-value content the brand can share — always ask permission before reposting, then share with credit. Make the client the headline; Maduuka is the supporting mention.

**Principle 6 — Crisis response**
A crisis is defined as: a data security incident, a major product outage affecting multiple clients, a harmful public statement about the brand, or a coordinated negative review campaign. If any of these occur, escalate immediately to the founder. Post no public response without the founder's direct approval. For full crisis communications protocol, apply the `playbook-crisis-communications` skill.

## 8. Paid Social Integration

Maduuka's paid budget is UGX 600,000 per month (Medium tier).

**Post boosting criteria:** Only boost a post when it meets all three of these conditions: (1) it has a clear, specific CTA (book a demo, start a trial), (2) it has already received organic engagement that suggests the message is resonating, and (3) the target audience can be defined with precision (business owners in Kampala, Uganda; pharmacy owners in Uganda; restaurant owners in Uganda). Never boost a post just because it exists.

**Recommended Phase 1 allocation:**

| Platform | Allocation | Purpose | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| Facebook / Instagram | UGX 400,000 | Lead generation targeting retail and hospitality SME owners in Kampala and major towns | Lead gen ads with demo booking CTA; boosted high-performing organic posts |
| LinkedIn | UGX 150,000 | Sponsored posts reaching business owners and managers in Uganda by job title | Sponsored content targeting SME owners and managing directors in Uganda |
| Reserve | UGX 50,000 | Opportunistic boosting of unexpectedly strong organic posts | Flexible |

**Budget rules:**
- Minimum 7-day campaign run — shorter runs do not generate enough data to optimise
- Review performance every two weeks, not monthly; at this budget level, wasted spend is felt immediately
- Document every paid conversion: if a campaign generates a demo booking that converts to a paying client, calculate the cost per acquisition and use it to justify a Phase 2 budget increase

**When to escalate from boosting to structured campaigns:** When monthly demo bookings from paid social exceed 10, move from ad-hoc boosting to a structured campaign using the `09-campaign-strategy` skill. Phase 1 does not require this.

## 10. Ninety-Day Milestone Roadmap

### Month 1 — Launch and First Clients (Days 1–30)

**Theme:** Build the foundation, convert the warm leads, get the first paying clients.

**Actions:**
1. Claim all Maduuka handles on LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and X — complete within Week 1
2. Set up LinkedIn Company Page using the 13-step checklist from `platform-linkedin-company-pages`
3. Set up Instagram, Facebook Page, and X with complete profiles
4. Set up WhatsApp Business number and auto-reply for demo enquiries
5. Set up demo booking link (Calendly) and basic email capture (Mailchimp)
6. Contact both waitlist businesses (bar and pharmacy) via WhatsApp — offer personal demo — close at least one as a paying client by Day 14
7. Publish the first week of content across LinkedIn (Peter personal + Maduuka Company Page) and Instagram — use the 30-day content plan in `platform-linkedin-company-pages` and `11-content-calendar` as templates
8. Begin first paid Facebook/Instagram lead generation campaign targeting retail and hospitality business owners in Kampala — UGX 200,000 for the first two weeks as a test

**Month 1 milestone:** At least 3 paying clients signed up. LinkedIn Company Page and Instagram both active with consistent posting. WhatsApp Business set up and responding. Demo booking link live and working.

---

### Month 2 — Consistency and Growth (Days 31–60)

**Theme:** Establish posting rhythm, use the first clients for social proof, scale what is working.

**Actions:**
1. Publish a case study post using the first paying client's story (with permission) — this is the single most valuable piece of content in the first 90 days
2. Launch LinkedIn Invite campaign using the 100-credit system to invite relevant first-degree connections to follow the Maduuka Company Page
3. Review Month 1 paid campaign performance — adjust targeting based on which ad set produced the most demo requests
4. Begin posting EFRIS education content series (minimum 4 pieces) — this builds search visibility and trust independently of product promotion
5. Activate Peter's personal LinkedIn thought leadership posting consistently (4 posts per week) using the platform-linkedin 30-day plan structure
6. Begin building email list — add email capture to the website; offer a free EFRIS compliance checklist download as the lead magnet
7. Post at least one Instagram Reel showing the product in use — screen recording of a closing-time report, with real data

**Month 2 milestone:** 10 paying clients total. First case study post published and performing. EFRIS education series launched. 100+ LinkedIn Company Page followers. Email list begun.

---

### Month 3 — Optimise and Pre-iOS Build-up (Days 61–90)

**Theme:** Hit the 20-client target; review what is working; begin soft iOS anticipation content.

**Actions:**
1. Review all platform analytics from Months 1–2 — identify top 3 posts by engagement and top 3 by conversion; produce more of both
2. Produce first monthly performance report using `meta-reporting` skill — document cost per acquisition from paid campaigns
3. Run second EFRIS education content batch — at the 60-day mark, EFRIS content will have built enough context to drive its own search and social organic reach
4. Begin very soft iOS anticipation content — one post on LinkedIn and one on Instagram by Day 85: "Maduuka is coming to iPhone. Here's what's being built." No promises. No launch date. Just an authentic behind-the-scenes signal.
5. Set up LinkedIn Newsletter with first issue — topic: "Running a business in Kampala: what the numbers actually tell you." This builds a subscriber list independent of the algorithm.
6. Confirm iOS launch month with product team — start detailed Phase 2 planning

**Month 3 milestone:** 20 paying clients. First performance report completed. Platform analytics reviewed and content mix adjusted. iOS announcement content drafted and scheduled. Phase 2 plan ready.
