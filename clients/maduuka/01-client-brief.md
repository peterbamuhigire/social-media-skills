## 1. Business Overview

**Industry:** SaaS — SME business management software (ERP/POS/bookkeeping)

**Stage:** Pre-revenue — product live on web and Android; iOS in development

**What Maduuka does:** A cloud-based ERP and point-of-sale platform built for African SMBs. Covers stock management, sales and invoicing, expense tracking, bookkeeping, and EFRIS (Uganda Revenue Authority) compliance. Accessible on web and Android; iOS launching mid-to-late Q3 2026.

**Geographic focus:** Uganda (Kampala primary); broader East African expansion in subsequent phases.

---

## 2. Unique Selling Proposition

Maduuka is built for African business reality — not adapted from a Western system. It handles EFRIS compliance natively, works on affordable Android devices with intermittent connectivity, and is priced for SME margins. Unlike a bespoke system built by a local developer, it is a continuously developed product with a support team, a roadmap, and no single-person dependency risk.

**Core USP statement:** African SMBs deserve software that was built for them, not translated for them. Maduuka runs where they run, speaks the language of their compliance obligations, and does not disappear when the developer finds a better client.

## 8. Competitive Landscape

**Reframed competitive set:** The real competition for Maduuka's typical client is not Sage or ERPNext. It is:

| Competitor type | Description | Their weakness |
|---|---|---|
| Local developer / IT consultant | Custom-built systems, bespoke solutions, WhatsApp support | Single-person dependency; no roadmap; client stranded when developer leaves |
| Pirated desktop POS | Unlicensed Sage, QuickBooks, or local Windows desktop software | No updates, no support, no backup, legal risk, no mobile access |
| EFRIS consultants | Help businesses configure URA compliance but solve only one problem | Leaves the rest of the business running manually |

**Global competitors (brand recognition, not daily competition):**
ERPNext, Sage Africa, Loyverse, Odoo, Shopify — these are comparison benchmarks an educated buyer might reference, but not the daily alternative most SME owners consider.

**Competitive messaging implication:** Do not position Maduuka against Sage or SAP in social content. The average SME owner in Kampala is choosing between Maduuka and "my nephew who set up something on an old laptop." Frame accordingly.

**EFRIS angle:** EFRIS compliance is a live, pressing pain point. Content that educates business owners about EFRIS requirements — regardless of whether they buy Maduuka — builds trust and positions the brand as the knowledgeable option in this space.

## 12. Admired Brands and Brands to Avoid

**Admired:**
- Shopify — merchant-first content; the product is the enabler, not the hero
- Flutterwave — speaks to African tech and business with genuine confidence; specific and credible
- Notion — one clear idea per post; exceptional consistency; design reflects the product's simplicity

**Avoid:**
- Sage / SAP Africa — corporate distance, jargon-heavy, speaks to CFOs not business owners
- Generic Ugandan telecom promo style (MTN/Airtel) — loud, exclamation-heavy, promotion-obsessed, treats customers as transactions
- Western SaaS startup hype — "disrupting," "10x your growth," "game-changing" — hollow and culturally misaligned
