## 2. Content Strategy

Instagram for Maduuka serves three purposes:
1. **Discovery** — Reels and carousel posts that reach new audiences through the algorithm and hashtags
2. **Education** — Carousels that teach EFRIS compliance and business management (searchable, saveable)
3. **Conversion** — Profile visits from engaged prospects who then click the link in bio to book a demo

Apply the Know, Like and Trust framework (Raymond and Johnston, 2021):
- Know content (Big Picture): EFRIS explainers, business management carousels, industry insight
- Like content (Human Interest): behind-the-scenes, founder content, client stories, real Kampala contexts
- Trust content (Products & Services): product Reels, feature demonstrations, pricing carousel

**Content mix for Instagram:**

| Format | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Reels (30–90 seconds) | 2–3 per week | Discovery and product demonstration; highest organic reach format on Instagram |
| Carousels (5–10 slides) | 1–2 per week | Education and save-worthy content; high dwell time signals quality to algorithm |
| Single image posts | 1 per week | Brand announcements, milestone posts, quote graphics |
| Stories | Daily (or near-daily) | Consistency signal; polls, behind-the-scenes, feature reveals, client check-ins |

## 3. Reels Strategy

Reels are the highest-priority Instagram format for Maduuka in Phase 1. They are the most effective format for:
- Reaching new audiences who do not yet follow @maduuka
- Demonstrating the product in under 60 seconds (the clearest conversion tool available)
- Showing real Kampala business contexts that build authentic recognition

**Reel production approach:**
- Screen recordings of the Maduuka app on Android: stock management, EFRIS receipt generation, end-of-day reports, pharmacy drug register, restaurant closing report
- Text overlays explaining each step (no voiceover required — 60–80% of Instagram Reels are watched without sound)
- Captions as teasers, not summaries — the first line earns the watch; it does not repeat what the Reel shows
- Subtitles for any spoken audio

**Reel hook formula (first 2 seconds must earn attention):**
- Problem-first: "Your cashier is leaving in 20 minutes. Do you know what you made today?" [then show the closing report]
- Observation-first: "This is what a Kampala pharmacy's EFRIS receipt looks like — in Maduuka." [then show it]
- Before/after: "Stock count used to take all Sunday. Now it takes this long." [then show the 3-minute count]

**Posting time for Reels:** Monday, Wednesday, and Friday evenings (7–9pm EAT) — peak Instagram usage time in Uganda.

## 4. Carousel Strategy

Carousels are the highest-save format on Instagram. A saved carousel means the audience member wants to return to it — this is the signal that the content is genuinely useful, and it is the metric that matters most for the Business Owner's Edge pillar.

**Carousel structure:**
- Slide 1 (cover): Bold hook text on branded background. Must work as a standalone image if someone never swipes.
- Slides 2–[N-1]: One idea per slide. Short text. Supporting visual (icon, simple graphic, screenshot).
- Final slide: CTA — "Save this / Share this / Free trial at maduuka.com — link in bio."

**Carousel topics for Phase 1:**
1. EFRIS in plain English (5 slides) — what it is, who it applies to, what a receipt must contain, what happens if non-compliant, one action to take this week
2. What a Kampala restaurant's closing report shows (6 slides) — food cost, covers, revenue, best item, payment breakdown
3. The 4 records NDA inspectors check first in a pharmacy (5 slides)
4. Maduuka in 5 slides: what it does, what it costs, how to start (the conversion carousel — always pinned to the profile)
5. A day in the life of a Maduuka user (6 slides) — before/after narrative across a business day

## 5. Stories Strategy

Stories provide daily presence without requiring the production effort of a Reel or carousel. They signal an active, consistent account to both the algorithm and the audience.

**Daily Stories cadence:**
- Monday: Teaser for the week's content ("Three posts this week — here is what's coming")
- Tuesday/Thursday: Behind-the-scenes — a real product build moment, a team interaction, a founder working
- Wednesday: Reshare of a strong comment or DM (anonymised if needed) — "Someone asked us this today..." [answer in the Story]
- Friday: Feature reveal — "Did you know Maduuka can do this?" [one feature, one screen recording clip]
- Weekend: Poll — audience question, product survey, "tell us what content you want next week"

**Archived Highlights:** Every EFRIS education Story is saved to the "EFRIS Guide" Highlight. Every product demo Story is saved to "What We Do." Over 90 days, these Highlights become a searchable product education library accessible from the profile without needing to post new content.

## 7. Cross-Posting and Amplification

**Peter's personal Instagram:** If Peter has an existing Instagram following, he should share Maduuka content to his Story 2–3 times per week. A Story share from a personal account with followers extends reach to audiences who have never seen @maduuka.

**Reposting client content:** As soon as clients begin tagging @maduuka in their own posts (after demo completion, after a first week of use, after a milestone), reshare immediately to @maduuka Stories and ask permission to feature in a dedicated post. Client-generated content is the highest-trust content on any platform.

**Instagram to WhatsApp pipeline:** Every Instagram post with a demo CTA should route to the link in bio, which includes a direct WhatsApp link. The conversion path is: Instagram post → profile visit → link in bio → WhatsApp or demo booking. Track where drop-off occurs.

## 8. Paid Instagram Strategy

**Budget allocation:** UGX 150,000–200,000 of the monthly UGX 400,000 Facebook/Instagram allocation should run as Instagram-specific ads.

**Phase 1 ad format:** Reel boosting — take a high-performing organic Reel (the product demo or EFRIS explainer) and boost it for 7 days targeting:
- Age: 25–50
- Location: Kampala and major Ugandan towns (Jinja, Mbarara, Gulu, Mbale)
- Interests: small business, retail, restaurant, pharmacy, accounting, business management
- Behaviour: business page administrators (Meta audience targeting)

**CTA on Instagram ads:** "Learn more" → maduuka.com, or "Send message" → WhatsApp. Test both; keep the CTA that generates more demo bookings.

**Never boost a post that does not have a clear CTA.** A lifestyle or behind-the-scenes post should not be boosted for conversion goals in Phase 1.

## 9. Instagram-Specific KPIs

| KPI | Day-30 Target | Day-90 Target | How to Measure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Followers | 50+ | 150+ | Instagram Insights |
| Reel plays | 500+ cumulative | 5,000+ cumulative | Instagram Insights |
| Carousel saves | 20+ per strong post | Growing | Instagram Insights |
| Profile visits | 200+ cumulative | 1,000+ cumulative | Instagram Insights |
| Link in bio clicks | 30+ | 100+ | Link aggregator analytics |
| Demo bookings from Instagram | 3+ | 10+ | Ask every lead "how did you find us?" |
| Story views | 30+ per Story | 100+ per Story | Instagram Insights |

**Review trigger:** If Reels are getting high play counts but low profile visits, the hook is working but the CTA or bio link flow is broken — review the bio link and the end frame of the Reel. If carousels are getting high saves but no demo bookings, the education content is resonating but conversion is not happening — add a harder CTA on the final slide and review the pricing carousel visibility.
