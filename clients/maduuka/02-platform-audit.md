# Maduuka — Platform Launch Audit
**Skill:** 02-platform-audit (adapted for clean-slate launch)
**Date:** March 2026
**Note:** Maduuka has no existing social media presence. This document functions as a launch readiness audit rather than a retrospective performance audit. Section 1 covers what must be set up. Section 2 benchmarks named competitors. Section 3 lists 10 first-week actions. Section 4 is the 6-slide deck outline.

*All competitor data is collected via manual public profile review and Meta Ad Library. Engagement rates are estimated using the formula: (total likes + comments on last 10 posts) ÷ (follower count × 10) × 100. All estimates are labelled as such. No competitor strategy or revenue is inferred from public data alone.*

## Output 4: Six-Slide Deck Outline

*For presenting Maduuka's social media launch plan to a stakeholder or investor audience.*

---

**Slide 1 — Situation: Where We Stand Today**
**Headline:** Maduuka is live — the social media strategy that takes us to 20 paying clients
**Bullets:**
- Product is live on Android and web; iOS coming Q3 2026
- Zero social media presence today — clean slate, full opportunity
- 90-day goal: 20 paying clients through content-led lead generation
- Platform strategy: LinkedIn + Instagram primary; Facebook paid; X secondary
**Speaker Notes:** Set the frame. This is not a brand awareness exercise — it is a 90-day client acquisition programme running through social media. Every decision is evaluated against "does this get us closer to a paying client?"
**Visual Direction:** Clean title slide. Maduuka logo, blue brand background, one headline stat ("0 to 20: the 90-day plan"), date.

---

**Slide 2 — The Competitive Landscape**
**Headline:** Our real competition does not have a social media strategy — that is the opportunity
**Bullets:**
- Named global competitors (Sage, ERPNext, Odoo) have large followings but zero Uganda-specific content
- Real daily competition: local developers and pirated desktop POS — no social presence at all
- EFRIS compliance is a live, urgent pain point with no clear social media owner
- Maduuka can own the EFRIS conversation in Uganda from day one
**Speaker Notes:** The competitive analysis reveals the most important insight: the real alternatives to Maduuka (local IT consultants, pirated Windows POS) do not run social media. Maduuka is not fighting for share of voice against Odoo — it is the only credible, modern option that speaks to Ugandan SME owners in their own context.
**Visual Direction:** Two-column comparison. Left: "What they have" (global brand logos, generic Africa content). Right: "What Maduuka has" (Uganda-specific, EFRIS-knowledgeable, locally built). Keep it factual and confident, not dismissive.

---

**Slide 3 — The Three-Pillar Content Strategy**
**Headline:** Every post serves one of three purposes: build trust, show proof, or explain the product
**Bullets:**
- Pillar 1: The Business Owner's Edge (40%) — EFRIS education, business management tips, Kampala-specific insight
- Pillar 2: Real Business Stories (30%) — client case studies, before/after narratives, founder-visits-client content
- Pillar 3: Inside Maduuka (30%) — product demos, feature explainers, team content
- 10-4-1 ratio applied: 10 value posts, 4 brand posts, 1 promotional CTA per 15 posts
**Speaker Notes:** The pillar structure prevents the two failure modes of startup social media: all product promotion (which produces unfollows) and all inspiration (which produces followers who never buy). The 10-4-1 rule is the operating discipline. Reference Bodnar and Cohen (2012).
**Visual Direction:** Three-column layout. One column per pillar with icon, percentage, and one example post type. Colour-coded by pillar: deep blue, warm amber, white/light.

---

**Slide 4 — Platform Strategy and Posting Plan**
**Headline:** LinkedIn for decisions, Instagram for discovery, Facebook for reach — WhatsApp for conversion
**Bullets:**
- LinkedIn (Maduuka + Peter personal): 7–8 posts/week combined — primary B2B lead generation
- Instagram: 4–5 posts/week — product demonstrations, client stories, Reels
- Facebook: 3 posts/week organic + UGX 400,000/month paid lead gen targeting Kampala SME owners
- X: 3 posts/week — EFRIS commentary, business discourse, regulatory insight
- WhatsApp: every demo enquiry and trial sign-up routes here — the conversion channel
**Speaker Notes:** The platform strategy follows the three personas. Patrick (retailer) is on Facebook. Amina (restaurant) is on Instagram. Dr. Joseph (pharmacist) is on LinkedIn. Peter's personal LinkedIn account is the most valuable reach asset in Phase 1 — it already has an audience and credibility that the new Maduuka Company Page cannot match immediately.
**Visual Direction:** Platform icons with posting frequency and primary persona association. A simple flow diagram: Content → Platform → Persona → Action (WhatsApp/demo booking).

---

**Slide 5 — The Ninety-Day Roadmap**
**Headline:** Month 1 closes the first clients; Month 2 builds proof; Month 3 hits the target
**Bullets:**
- Month 1: Claim handles, set up profiles, contact waitlist, publish first content, launch paid ads — target: 3 paying clients by Day 30
- Month 2: Publish first case study, launch LinkedIn invite campaign, optimise paid targeting — target: 10 paying clients by Day 60
- Month 3: Hit 20-client target, produce first performance report, begin soft iOS anticipation content — target: 20 paying clients by Day 90
- iOS launch phase begins Day 91: 4–6 weeks of build-up campaign content, full launch on iOS launch day
**Speaker Notes:** The milestones are deliberately specific and measurable. If Month 1 closes only 1 client rather than 3, the strategy should be reviewed at Day 30 — not at Day 90. Early signals matter. The iOS launch in Q3 2026 is the next major campaign event; Phase 2 planning begins at the Day 60 review.
**Visual Direction:** Three-column timeline (Month 1 / Month 2 / Month 3). Each column has theme, 3 key actions, and milestone. Clean, linear, specific.

---

**Slide 6 — KPIs and What Success Looks Like**
**Headline:** One number matters most: 20 paying clients by Day 90
**Bullets:**
- Primary: 20 paying clients before iOS launch — tracked weekly
- Demo requests: 30+ cumulative over 90 days (leading indicator for paying clients)
- LinkedIn Company Page: 200 followers, 3%+ engagement rate by Day 90
- Instagram: 150 followers, 4%+ engagement rate by Day 90
- Cost per acquisition (paid): document every client traced to a paid campaign to justify Phase 2 budget
**Speaker Notes:** The KPI framework applies the RACE model (Chaffey, 2024): Reach (impressions, followers), Act (engagement, clicks), Convert (demo requests, trial sign-ups, paying clients), Engage (repeat engagement, WhatsApp enquiries). All baselines are zero. Targets are 90-day SMART builds. The single number that matters is paying clients. If reach and engagement are strong but paying clients are not converting, the problem is the demo process or the product, not the content.
**Visual Direction:** Two-tier KPI layout. Top tier (large): "20 paying clients — Day 90 target". Bottom tier (smaller): supporting metrics in a 2×3 grid. Use Maduuka brand blue for the primary metric; grey for supporting metrics.
